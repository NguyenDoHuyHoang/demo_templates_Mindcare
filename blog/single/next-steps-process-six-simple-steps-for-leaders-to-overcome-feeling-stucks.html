Next Steps Process: Six Simple Steps for Leaders to Overcome Feeling Stucks

 Coach Arnold Murray       February 19, 2025

Have you ever felt stuck when you needed to make a meaningful decision? The more significant the decision, the more tempted we feel to have a fully developed strategy before we do anything. The truth is you don't need to have everything figured out to move forward. As a coach for leaders connected to their spiritual mission, I've discovered that one of the biggest challenges they face is trying to map out their entire journey all at once.

Instead of trying to figure out everything up front, I help leaders focus on something much simpler and more powerful - taking the next step.

I call this my Next Steps process. Here are six different types of steps you can take, and how understanding these options can make decision-making much clearer and less overwhelming.

        1. Stepping Out: Expanding Your Horizons

Let's start with "Stepping Out." This is about moving beyond your current circles and comfort zones. But here's what makes it different from a complete life change - you're not abandoning everything you know, you're just extending your reach.

When considering if Stepping Out is your next move, ask yourself:

- Are you feeling constrained by your current environment?

- Do people outside your usual circles keep asking for your help or expertise?

- Are you curious about applying your skills in new contexts?

- Do you find yourself thinking "I could do more if I just had a broader platform"?

The beauty of Stepping Out is that it doesn't require a complete transformation. You might keep your current role while starting to offer your services to new audiences. You might maintain your existing business while exploring new markets. It's about expansion, not abandonment.

        2. Stepping In: Deepening Your Impact

"Stepping In" is often overlooked because it's subtle, but it can be incredibly powerful. This is about going deeper where you already are, finding new opportunities within your current environment.

When might Stepping In be your best next step? Consider these indicators:

- Do you see untapped potential in your current role or organization?

- Are there problems around you that you know how to solve, but haven't taken on yet?

- Do you sense there's more you could learn or contribute where you are?

- Are there opportunities for growth that you've been hesitant to embrace?

Stepping In is particularly powerful because it builds on existing foundations. You already understand the context, the relationships, the challenges. You're not starting from scratch - you're leveraging what you already know to create new value.

        3. Stepping Up: Increasing Your Commitment

"Stepping Up" is about increased commitment and intentionality in areas where you're already involved. This isn't about doing new things - it's about doing current things with greater purpose and impact.

Consider Stepping Up when:

- You're already successful in an area but operating below your potential

- You see opportunities to take more responsibility

- You're ready to move from an individual contributor to leader

- You want to deepen your impact in a field you care about

The key difference between Stepping Up and other steps is that you're not changing direction - you're increasing intensity. It's about saying "I'm going to do this better, stronger, with more focus and commitment."

        4. Stepping Back: Gaining Perspective

"Stepping Back" might sound like retreat, but it's actually about strategic positioning. This step is crucial for clarity and long-term success. Sometimes, the best way to move forward is to temporarily step back.

You might need to Step Back when:

- You're feeling overwhelmed or burned out

- Your decisions are reactive rather than strategic

- You need time to develop new skills or perspectives

- Your current pace isn't sustainable

- You need clarity about your next direction

Remember, Stepping Back isn't giving up - it's getting perspective. It's creating space for reflection and renewal. Often, this step leads to better decisions and stronger future moves.

        5. Stepping Down: Strategic Release

"Stepping Down" is perhaps the most misunderstood step. People often see it as failure, but strategic stepping down can be a powerful move forward. This is about intentionally releasing responsibilities or roles that no longer serve your broader purpose.

Consider Stepping Down when:

- Your current role is preventing growth in more important areas

- You're holding onto something out of obligation rather than purpose

- Someone else could do better in your current position

- You need to create space for new opportunities

- Your energy would be better invested elsewhere

The key to Stepping Down successfully is recognizing that it's not about losing something - it's about creating space for something better.

        6. Stepping Away: Making a Clean Break

Finally, there's "Stepping Away" - making a clean break. While this might sound similar to Stepping Down, it's different. Stepping Away is about complete disengagement, often necessary when:

- A situation is no longer healthy or productive

- You've invested time and energy, but it's not leading where you need to go

- Continuing would mean compromising your values or well-being

- The cost of staying outweighs any potential future benefits

The courage to Step Away often opens doors to entirely new opportunities that wouldn't be visible otherwise.

        Moving Forward

When you're feeling stuck about your next move, don't try to figure out your entire life path. Instead, ask yourself: Which of these six steps makes the most sense right now?

By focusing on just the next step rather than the entire journey, you make decision-making manageable. You reduce overwhelm. And most importantly, you start moving forward instead of staying stuck in analysis paralysis.

Success isn't about having your entire path figured out. It's about taking thoughtful, intentional steps in the right direction. Sometimes, just knowing what kind of step you need to take is enough to get you moving again.
