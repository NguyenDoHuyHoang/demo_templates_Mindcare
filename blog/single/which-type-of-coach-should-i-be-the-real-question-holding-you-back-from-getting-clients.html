“Which Type of Coach Should I Be?” — The Real Question Holding You Back from Getting Clients

If you’ve recently become a certified coach, first of all—congratulations. You’ve taken a bold step toward building a purpose-driven business that changes lives. But after the initial excitement fades, one big question starts to creep in…

“Which type of coach should I be?”

It’s a question that feels deceptively simple—but underneath, it’s loaded with self-doubt, imposter syndrome, and the fear of making the wrong choice.

You might be thinking:

  • What if I choose the wrong niche?

  • What if nobody books me?

  • What if I’m not “expert enough” to coach others?

  • Where do I even start finding clients?

If this sounds familiar, you're not alone—and this blog is exactly what you need right now.

Why “Which Type of Coach Should I Be?” Feels So Overwhelming

New coaches often face two major roadblocks at the beginning:

  1. The pressure to “niche down” immediately
     You've heard it over and over again: "The riches are in the niches." But when you're just getting started, locking yourself into one niche can feel restrictive—or downright terrifying. You're still learning what you love, where you thrive, and who you enjoy helping most.

  2. The confusion around how to find paying clients
     You’ve built the skillset, got the certification… but now what? You might be posting on Instagram, attending networking events, or sending out feelers, but nothing is landing. And without a clear niche, you're unsure how to market yourself at all.

This creates a vicious cycle:
 No niche → no clear message → no clients → self-doubt → paralysis.

But here's the truth: you don’t need to have it all figured out before you start coaching.

Why It’s Okay Not to Know Your Exact Niche (Yet)

Let’s flip the script.

Instead of obsessing over the perfect niche, what if you gave yourself permission to explore?

Because the best way to discover your ideal niche isn’t through brainstorming—it’s through doing.

  • Taking on real clients

  • Coaching across a few categories

  • Seeing who lights you up

  • Watching what problems people keep coming to you for

That’s when your niche reveals itself—not when you're staring at a blank journal page trying to manifest it.
